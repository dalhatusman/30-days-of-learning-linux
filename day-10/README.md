# Day 10 - Linux Log Management 

## Objective

What was the goal for today? To understand how Linux logs system activities and how to use logs for monitoring and troubleshooting.

---

## What I Learned

- Linux stores system activity in log files, mainly located in /var/log/.
- Logs help in debugging errors, monitoring systems, and auditing activities. 

---

## What I Built / Practiced


- Learned that log access is permission-based, and administrative privileges (sudo) are often required to view or monitor system logs. 

---

## Challenges Faced

- Could not access most log files due to lack of permissions (no sudo privileges).

---

## Key Takeaways

- Logs are the first place to check when something goes wrong.
- tail -f is powerful for real-time debugging.
- Combining grep with logs makes troubleshooting faster.
